Aim

To understand and verify the chain rule for partial differentiation of a composite function of two variables.

The experiment studies functions of the form f = f(u, v), where u = g(x, y) and v = h(x, y). Students compute partial derivatives with respect to x and y, compare symbolic and numerical results, and visualize the behavior of the function and its derivatives.

Theory

If a function f depends on intermediate variables u and v, and those variables depend on x and y, then f is indirectly a function of x and y.

The chain rule helps us find the rate of change of f with respect to x and y through the intermediate variables.

Composite Function

f = f(u, v), where u = g(x, y) and v = h(x, y)

Chain Rule Formulae

df/dx = (df/du)(du/dx) + (df/dv)(dv/dx)
df/dy = (df/du)(du/dy) + (df/dv)(dv/dy)

Procedure

1. Choose or enter f(u, v), u = g(x, y), and v = h(x, y).

2. Find the six basic partial derivatives: df/du, df/dv, du/dx, du/dy, dv/dx, and dv/dy.

3. Substitute these values in the chain rule formula.

4. Replace u and v with their expressions in x and y.

5. Evaluate at a point and compare with numerical central-difference derivatives.
